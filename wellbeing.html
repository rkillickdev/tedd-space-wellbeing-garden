A dose of Vitamin G

Several studies have shown that immersing ourselves in nature, or even just looking at it, is good for our mental and physical health. Researchers have discovered that a daily dose of Vitamin G (Green) can be highly effective in reducing stress, anxiety, depression and fatigue. Read on to learn more about some of the features we have incorporated into our wellbeing garden and how they can revitalise us.

Water

Research has shown that living near water has a positive effect on human health. We strive to harness its healing powers throughout the garden to leave you feeling restored and rejuvenated. The stillness of our wildlife pond provides a calm haven where you can reflect, be mindful and let nature fascinate you. Our paths are flanked by rills of trickling water that provide a soothing, mesmeric soundscape.

Scent

For centuries people have believed in the power of botanical scents to boost mood, health, and wellbeing. While it can have a strong emotive influence on us, it can also have therapeutic qualities and scientific studies have shown how scent can interact with brain receptors and neurotransmitters to promote relaxation. For example Lavender is known to have benefits as a mood and sleep aid, while Rosemary can be used to boost energy levels. Come and inhale deeply to discover how we have weaved scent throughout the garden.

Colour

Different colours can have a strong effect on our mood, so we have considered this in our planting schemes. We use a limited palette of cool tones to create a calm relaxing space. Greenery plays a very important role and features heavily, as studies show that people find this the most soothing colour. Many different hues are used with varying levels of saturation and brightness to keep the space visually interesting.
